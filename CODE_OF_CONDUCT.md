# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members of this community and contributors to its body of knowledge pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, place of origin, gender identity and expression, gender, level of experience, education, social-economic status, race, religion, skin color, sexual identity and orientation, and sexual preference.

We are committed to providing a welcoming and safe environment for all users, contributors, and community members.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contradicts this code of conduct include:
* Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
* Public or private harassment.
* Publishing private information about other community members ("doxing").
* Prolonged whining about problems without attempting to suggest and implement solutions.
* Welcome a variety of points of view and discourage any behavior that would discourage the participation of others.

## Our Responsibilities

### Community Managers
Community managers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and implementing fair and consistent moderating practices.

### Contributors
Contributors are expected to adhere to the standards outlined in this code of conduct.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive behavior will not be tolerated. The project team is free to remove, block, or ban any user who violates this code of conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, and also extends to all official project community social media channels, mailing lists, forums, and events.

## Reporting

If you encounter behavior that contradicts this code of conduct, please report it by opening an issue on the GitHub repository: [https://github.com/MohammadB88/MLOps-Workshop-Exercises/issues](https://github.com/MohammadB88/MLOps-Workshop-Exercises/issues)
